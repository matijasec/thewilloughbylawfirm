What We Handle

Serious Injuries
Deserve Serious Representation

We handle the full range of personal injury and civil rights cases in Houston — including cases other firms decline to take.

01
Motor Vehicle Accidents
Car crashes, DUI accidents, hit-and-runs, and multi-vehicle collisions throughout Houston and Harris County. We build complete accident reconstructions and pursue all liable parties.
02
18-Wheeler & Commercial Trucking
Federal regulations, hours-of-service violations, black-box data, and carrier liability require specialized knowledge. We know trucking cases inside and out.
03
Civil Rights Violations — § 1983
Excessive force, unlawful arrest, and Monell claims against government entities in federal court. We hold agencies accountable for constitutional violations.
04
Government Entity Claims (TTCA)
Crashes involving city and county vehicles are governed by strict notice deadlines under the Texas Tort Claims Act. Call us before those deadlines pass.
05
Wrongful Death
When a loved one is lost to another's negligence, we pursue the full value of their life — past and future — for the family left behind.
06
Workplace & Premises Injuries
Construction accidents, unsafe properties, and on-the-job injuries. We identify every avenue of recovery beyond basic workers' compensation.

The Firm

A Different Kind of Personal Injury Firm

The Willoughby Law Firm was built on a simple principle: injured people deserve a lawyer who prepares for trial — not one who churns settlements. Joshua Willoughby brings that trial-ready mentality to every case, from the first client call to the final verdict or settlement.

Based in Houston's Midtown, Josh handles a focused docket of personal injury and civil rights cases — which means your case doesn't get lost in a volume practice. He knows your file. He answers your questions. And when the insurance company plays hardball, he plays harder.

His practice spans motor vehicle crashes, commercial trucking cases, § 1983 civil rights claims in federal court, and government entity claims under the Texas Tort Claims Act.
